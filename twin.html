Narrative Guide: How to Explain April to the Board

The signal under the noise: Organic ABC core (Sub + Usage ex-AI) grew ~18% MoM in April — from $15.4M to $17.2M (excl. Pro catch-up and AI). The April number looks bad at -18% because March was artificially inflated by Blackbox.ai AI Gateway spike ($2.9M → $0.2M), and April was simultaneously hit by a -$9.3M AI reclassification, -$0.4M ProServ rev rec change, and -$1.3M FLOW-related opex load.

Gross margin: 50.3% in April vs 63.1% in March. The decline is structural (v0 Fluid CPU costs +66% MoM, Turbo builds +131%) but partially temporary. Migration to Hive should improve v0 margins. Excluding v0 and AI, core GM was 65.6% — essentially flat.

EBITDA: -$17.2M is the worst in the dataset, but ~$5.3M of that is FLOW event costs, ~$2.7M is the SBC double-accrual, and ~$3M is the Pro catch-up margin impact. Normalised ongoing burn is closer to $10–11M/month, consistent with Feb-Mar.

The $534M cash position provides 50 months of normalised runway — there is no liquidity concern. The question is the path to profitability, which requires v0 margin improvement and AI Gateway revenue stabilisation.
