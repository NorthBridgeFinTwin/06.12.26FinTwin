Design principles

What I believe about finance systems

  • 01
    The ERP is not an accounting tool — it is the operating system of the company
    Every business decision flows through the ERP sooner or later — revenue recognition, headcount cost allocation, procurement approval, cash position. When the ERP is well-architected, the whole company moves faster. When it's patched together with workarounds, every team builds shadow systems and the finance close becomes archaeology.
  • 02
    AI agents belong inside the close, not after it
    Most companies use AI to summarise what already happened. The better use is to run it during the close itself — catching anomalies as journal entries post, validating reconciliations before sign-off, flagging SOX control deviations in real time. The agent that prevents the error is worth ten that explain it.
  • 03
    Integration debt is financial risk
    Every manual data transfer between systems — every CSV export, every copy-paste between billing and ERP, every spreadsheet that "reconciles" two systems — is a control risk, an audit exposure, and a close delay waiting to happen. Closing integration gaps is not an IT project; it is a financial controls imperative.
  • 04
    SOX is a discipline, not a deadline
    Companies that treat SOX compliance as a Q4 scramble build fragile control environments that break under growth. The ones that embed controls into system configuration — access provisioning, change management workflows, automated evidence collection — barely notice audit season because every day is audit-ready.
  • 05
    Systems adoption is the real project
    A perfectly configured ERP that the accounting team works around is a failed implementation. The technical build is 40% of the work. Change management — training, process documentation, adoption measurement, and making the new system easier than the old spreadsheet — is the other 60%. The measure of success is zero shadow systems, not go-live day.
"The best finance system doesn't just record what happened — it prevents the error, closes the gap, and makes the next close faster than the last."
