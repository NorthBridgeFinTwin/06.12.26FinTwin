Key CapEx & FCF Insights
🏗️ The GPU Fleet Depreciation Cliff
NovaSpark capitalized $892M of GPU hardware over 12 months on a 36-month useful life. That means $24.8M/month of D&A is already baked in — and growing. By Dec-26, monthly D&A hits ~$68M. Unlike software companies, AI infra players carry GAAP losses long after reaching operational profitability. The board needs to track Cash EBITDA (pre-D&A), not GAAP net income.
⚡ Utilization is the Only Lever That Matters
At 75% GPU utilization, gross margin is ~28%. At 90%, it's 38%. At 95%+ (theoretical max), it approaches 52%. Every percentage point of utilization improvement is worth ~$8M/year in gross profit at current scale. The H2-26 hyperscaler contract with a major LLM foundation lab locks in 85% utilization on 12,000 GB200 GPUs for 24 months — this single contract changes the FCF trajectory.
📦 CapEx Payback: 26 Months at Current Rates
An H100 cluster costing $40K/GPU generates ~$3.20/GPU-hour at market rates. At 90% utilization (7,884 hrs/yr), that's $25.2K annual revenue per GPU — a 63% annual yield. Subtract power ($4.2K/yr at $0.07/kWh), networking ($1.1K), and data center ($2.8K), and each GPU generates ~$17.1K of gross profit annually. Simple payback: ~28 months. That's the economics this model is built on.
🎯 FCF Inflection: Q1-27 is the Target
The model shows FCF turning positive in Feb-27, assuming: (1) the GB200 cluster reaches 88% utilization by Dec-26; (2) CapEx drops to $45M/mo from Jan-27 as major build-out completes; (3) Managed Inference API hits $35M/mo revenue at 61% GM. Miss any of these, and FCF breakeven slides to Q3-27 — burning an additional ~$320M of cash. This is the single most important forecast risk for the board.
